Best way to book a flight ticket

Jul 10, 2018 4 min read

Its been 2 years since I visited home (India). Due to my Teaching assistantships during previous Winter semesters, I couldn’t get a chance to visit during regular holiday season and moreover my research work kept me busy for most of the time. Finally, as I’m working halfway through the Masters thesis (writing) decided to visit home and if possible try to work from home. I thought to share my so called standard operating procedure to buy a flight ticket at ‘the’ best price. The procedure has two phases, first one is finding the dates combination (arrival and departure) that fits in our trip duration range and has the lowest fare possible. The second phase is to find coupons, promos, for a flight ticket with these dates.

Lets get started, I’ll be departing from Toronto (YYZ), the nearest Intl. airport from Waterloo that actually has flights to India and Bangalore (BLR) is the nearest airport to my hometown Pileru, Andhra Pradesh, India.

Assuming you have the flexibility with the dates +/- 10 days and looking for a best price. (Ofcourse, no 25 hour+ long flights). These are the two variables that decide the price of a roundtrip ticket,

  1. Departure date.
  2. Trip length in days.

Two years ago while I was booking flight ticket, I used to randomly pick a departure date and return date and check the price and then tweak the dates around to see if the price reduced and the trip duration is within the , it was very uncertain as the online websites doesn’t show any price matrix for different days and even if it shows. There’s this cumbersome process of going through each combination of departure date and trip length.

First stop is at the Google flights, “http://flights.google.com" Just fill in the Destination and starting airports, trip details like number of passengers and so on. Don’t worry about the dates for now. Click search

We can see a list of departing flights, flight insights tabs. The most useful according to me is the “Price graph”. Click “SEE MORE”, the tab will maximize and shows us a bar chart of prices for a given departure date and trip length. We can conveniently tweak these two parameters and check the price. So in my case, I started with a full month i.e., 30 day trip length and initial departure date of Septemeber 1. (you can enter the date in the top search bar next to the Destination and set the trip duration in the “Price Graph” window). I chose september 1 due to personal reasons that I can sublet my apartment during the month of travel. So my choice is to have the departure date as close to September 1 as possible.

Then I chose to reduce the trip duration by each day to see the change in price (keep noticing the y-axis as well), I found the 25 day trip duration to be most economical, each bar in this bar chart denotes the price for the given trip duration and departure date and as we move cursor through each bar to see the price values. I didn’t had to work that hard, the September 2 departure date is the lowest with 913$ roundtrip price. (within the range of +/- 10 days of Septmeber 1)

This is an important information for us, that date combination (Sept 2 - Sept. 27) is the best combination for us to have a cheap fare. Now, go to any online ticket selling website like expedia, trivago and so on and look for the airticket price for this dates. You may even ask your traveling agent for a price quote with these days.
